Who Am I

I am currently a senior studying Information Technology and Web Science at Rensselaer Polytechnic Institute, and plan on grauating in May of 2016. I primarily focus on frontend web development, with extending interests within community development, social media managmenet, and growth engineering.

My Current Project: Bundles

My current project, Bundles, is a simple web application that allows people to share multiple pieces of content with others with one single link. To get updates about the project follow the twitter account @makebundles and also subscribe to the mailing list.

Full Sites:

Status

Status is a small but ambitious project I decided to start in 2016 where I would publicly track my tasks, accomplishments, and other habits that I want to build for the year. I am making it public so that other people can peek in on how well I am doing with keeping up on my tasks. The site is very manual currently, but I hope to make it sort of dynamic in the near future.

Dollar

Another one of my current projects is Dollar. Dollar is a web application made to track expenses for those who prefer the checkbook method for tracking their finances. I developed Dollar to alleviate many of the problems I had with using a Google Doc spreadsheet to manage my expenses. You can follow the @usedollar twitter account for more information about when it will be available for launch!

Trends

Trends was an interesting project built to show the top 10 tweets in a specific area of news coverage. I learned a lot about developing with the Twitter API, as well as developing my own API to communicate with a flat file server.

How To Hustle

How to Hustle was a short lived side project that inspired me to write content that would allow me and other entreprenuers to focus and to hustle in their efforts to build things. I learned a lot about the difficulties writing content every week for an audience.

No Longer Working On:

RPIDelivery

RPIDelivery was an ambitious attempt at jumping into the food delivery scene. THe original plan was to have students text me their orders, I would then go buy the food they ordered and deliver it to them anywhere on campus. This project failed for two main reasons, the first is that I dramatically underestimated the amount of time that goes into handling food delivery for college students, the second is that I simply couldn't round up other students to help with the delivery. There were some other minor issues I ran into, for example how to handle fake orders.

It is important to realize however that our failures are sometimes more important than our successes, and that they should act as learning experiences.

Pace

Pace was a project that I developed partially with a team of developers from school, and then continued on after the school project ended. I gained a ton of experience working with real world customers and balancing a want vs a need when developing for other people.

Afternoon Coffee

Afternoon Coffee was one of the original public Slack chat groups, before #startup and other large slack communities. I started Afternoon Coffee to reach out to more developers and have conversations about ideas, methodologies, and centrally feedback on my thoughts.
